<p class="notice--primary">My research examines how communication processes are shaped by brain, behavior, and media context, with a particular emphasis on sleep neuroscience and computational social science methods.</p>

## Current directions

- Neurobiological explanations of human communication and media effects.
- Sleep neuroscience and the role of rest in cognition, perception, and communication.
- Computational and high-throughput approaches for studying social and communication behavior.
- Lab-based experiments, EEG, and fMRI as complementary methods.

## Methods I use

I work across experimental design and computational analysis, including EEG, fMRI, and lab-based studies. I am especially interested in research programs that connect mechanism with real-world communication behavior.
